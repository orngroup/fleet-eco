01 · THE DAILY ECONOMICS

€450 in. €750 out. The market pays for our fleet.

The market rate for a chauffeured touring van in Europe sits around €750 per day — the same benchmark as the ORN Connect coach rate in the WOE 2026 costing. By owning the vehicle and charging our own tours a discounted internal rate of €450/day, every tour instantly books its transport 40% cheaper, and the €450 flows straight back into fleet capital recovery rather than a supplier's pocket.

02 · SAVING ON AN AVERAGE TOUR

€3,900 per tour. €557 per seat.

Using our Wonders of Europe 2026 programme (13 days) as the reference tour: market van hire costs €9,750 per departure, the ORN fleet costs €5,850. For a private 7-seater departure, the transport cost per passenger falls from €1,393 to €836 — a €557 per-seat advantage we can take as margin, or use to price private-van Europe at a level no incumbent can follow.

07 · WHY OWN FLEET

They can copy an ad. Not the operating stack.

From both strategy documents, the van fleet is not a cost line — it is a distribution, product and margin asset:

Margin from every component

Incumbents buy coach capacity departure by departure. ORN earns the tour, the coach, the van, the hotel, the transfer and the excursion on the same passenger.

Premium product engine

The 7–8 seat V-Class is the vehicle behind ORN Signature, private van upgrades (€40k credit in Europe Access) and small-group premium touring — parity-or-above pricing on a €450/day cost base.

Ancillary revenue machine

Home Connect at £39–199 each way, airport and rail arrivals, regional feeders at £20–35 — all high-margin inventory that only exists because the van does.

Pricing weapon

The €300/day advantage funds entry-bucket fares incumbents cannot follow: if they cut price, their externally purchased cost base compresses their margin, not ours.

Network resilience

The strategy calls for 60–80 vans covering feeders, arrivals, premium touring and 8–10 vehicles of disruption reserve. Owned fleet turns disruption from a supplier negotiation into a dispatch decision.

Trust and brand control

"ORN coaches. ORN people. ORN ground." The vehicle is the most visible proof of the one-operator promise sold across Europe Access and the UK entry campaign.

08 · GUARDRAILS

Before the board signs.

Assumptions to lock down

  • What the €450/day covers. The payback above treats €450 as pure capital recovery. If fuel, insurance, maintenance, tolls and depreciation come out of it (typically €80–150/day for a V-Class, excluding driver), use the running-cost slider — at €120/day absorbed, payback stretches from 145 to ~197 charged days.
  • Drivers are a separate line. Employed vs freelance driver cost sits outside both the €450 and €750 figures and must be added to any per-tour costing — consistent with the WOE model where driver meals and accommodation are itemised separately.
  • Seasonality. The 40% annual utilisation target must be built from the monthly ramp (peak May–October, Christmas markets), not assumed flat. Winter feeder and airport work protects the average.
  • Contribution floors. Per the yield doctrine, no private-van departure sells below its approved minimum contribution after variable costs — the €450 rate is an internal transfer price, not a licence to discount the retail fare.
  • Residual value upside. Payback here assumes the van is worth zero at the end — in reality a 2–3 year old V-Class retains meaningful resale value, so true economics are better than shown.
